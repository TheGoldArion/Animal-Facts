Moon Jellies

Moon Jellies are part of the Jellyfish family. They are sometimes called common jellyfish, moon jellyfish, or saucer jelly. Surprisingly enough, they haven't been to the moon, although in 1991, scientists did send them into space aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia as part of an experiment. Moon Jellies, along with all Jellyfish, do not have a heart, lungs, brain or blood, and are made up of multiple organisms hat work together to thrive and survive.

Moon Jellies are bioluminescent, which means they can glow underwater.They are 95% water, and are just a floating mouth and digestive system. Fossils have been found of Moon Jellies, showing that they lived 500 million years ago. for context, that means they were floating around the ocean before the dinosaurs.
